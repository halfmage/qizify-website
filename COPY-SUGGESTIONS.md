# Copy Analysis & Suggestions: learnslice.com
**Date:** 2026-04-04
**Page Type:** Homepage + /companies landing page
**Copy Score:** 68/100

---

## Executive Summary

LearnSlice's copy has strong bones: a clear value proposition (apprentices job-ready in 8 weeks), concrete ROI numbers (€32K savings), and solid social proof (BMWE prize, university partnerships, named testimonials). The "How It Works" section is crisp and actionable.

However, the copy undersells the emotional urgency, leans too heavily on feature-speak in the mid-page sections, and has CTA inconsistencies that create friction. The biggest missed opportunity: the testimonials describe the tool rather than transformation — none include quantified results. The /companies page is stronger than the homepage because it leads with a bolder promise ("Turn Every Apprentice Into a Top Performer"), but the homepage headline buries the "who" (it never says who LearnSlice is for in the H1).

Priority fixes: (1) Sharpen the homepage headline to name the audience and the outcome, (2) rewrite testimonials to lead with results, (3) unify CTAs around one clear primary action.

## Value Proposition Analysis

```
TARGET CUSTOMER:  German companies with apprentices (Mittelstand HR leads + C-level)
PROBLEM:          Apprentice onboarding takes 4-6 months, trainers are overloaded,
                  30% drop out — costing €20K-€35K each
SOLUTION:         AI learning platform combining IHK exam prep + company knowledge
UNIQUE MECHANISM: AI performance catalyst that adapts 24/7 to each apprentice
KEY BENEFIT:      Job-ready in 8 weeks (not 6 months), €32K saved per apprentice
PROOF:            30+ companies, BMWE prize, FH Dortmund partnership, 3 testimonials
```

**Gaps identified:**
- **Problem is understated.** The page jumps to the solution without dwelling on the pain. Add a dedicated "The Problem" section before "How It Works."
- **Proof is thin on outcomes.** Testimonials praise the tool/team but cite zero measurable results. "We reduced onboarding from 5 months to 6 weeks" would be 10x more powerful than "We're very satisfied."
- **Unique mechanism is buried.** "AI Performance Catalyst" is mentioned but not explained in plain terms. What makes LearnSlice's AI different from ChatGPT + a PDF?

## Section-by-Section Copy Suggestions

### Hero Section

**Current subheadline:**
> "LearnSlice combines IHK exam preparation with your company knowledge into personalized AI learning paths, so your apprentices are job-ready in 8 weeks, not 6 months. Save €32K per apprentice and free up 50% of trainer time."

**Issue:** Too long (46 words). Tries to say everything at once. Subheadline should do ONE job: expand on the headline with a supporting benefit or mechanism.

**Recommended:**
> "AI learning paths that combine IHK exam prep with your company knowledge. Save €32K per apprentice and free up 50% of trainer time."

(27 words. Mechanism + two key numbers. The "8 weeks" is already in the headline.)

---

### ROI Section

**Current headline:** "€32K–€41K saved per young talent. Big savings, quick ROI."

**Issue:** "Big savings, quick ROI" is filler. It restates what the number already says.

**Recommended:** "€32K–€41K Saved Per Apprentice. Here's the Math."

**Why:** "Here's the Math" creates curiosity and frames the comparison table as proof, not marketing.

---

### How It Works

**Current: Strong.** The 3-step structure is clear and well-written. Minor improvements:

**Step 01 — current:** "Prebuilt curriculum mapped to regional IHK requirements: the complete content & learning path every Azubi needs to pass."

**Issue:** "the complete content & learning path every Azubi needs to pass" is vague. Pass what? The IHK exam — say it.

**Recommended:** "Prebuilt curriculum mapped to regional IHK requirements — everything your apprentice needs to pass their IHK exam. Zero curriculum work on your end."

---

### Use Cases Section

**Current headline:** "Made for industries that count"

**Issue:** Vague. "Industries that count" means nothing to the reader.

**Recommended:** "Built for Companies, Schools, and Agencies That Train Germany's Workforce"

**Why:** Names the three audiences and frames them as important (workforce training), not generic.

---

### Testimonials

**Current weakness:** All three testimonials describe the tool or the partnership. None mention a measurable outcome. This is the single highest-impact copy change available.

**Recommended approach:** Ask customers for one specific number and rewrite the pull quote around it. Example:

**Before (current):**
> "We're very satisfied with LearnSlice. The team quickly transformed our materials into precise, effective quizzes with high quality and efficiency."

**After (recommended, if data exists):**
> "Our apprentices passed the IHK exam on the first try for the first time in three years. LearnSlice transformed our training materials into targeted quizzes that actually close knowledge gaps."
> — Lienne Julie Wolff, Tremonia Mobility GmbH

If no outcome data exists yet, at minimum reframe around the *result felt*:

> "Our trainers finally have their weeks back. LearnSlice turned our 60-page manual into structured learning paths in days — now apprentices find answers themselves instead of interrupting the team."

---

### Trust Section

**Current:** "30+ German companies" — solid but generic.

**Recommended:** "Trusted by 30+ German companies — from Mittelstand manufacturers to vocational colleges." Adding "Mittelstand" signals the core audience.

---

### Final CTA

**Current:** "€4K saved per apprentice. €20K per 5 apprentices. No risk. Cancel anytime."

**Issue:** The savings numbers here (€4K) contradict the hero section (€32K). This creates confusion. Is it €4K or €32K?

**Recommended:** Either align the number or explain it. Likely the €4K is the platform cost savings, not total ROI. Rewrite:

"The platform pays for itself with one apprentice. With five, you save over €20K per year. No risk. Cancel anytime."

## CTA Optimization

### Current CTA Inventory

| CTA Text | Location | Issue |
|----------|----------|-------|
| "See It In Action" | Hero | Vague — see *what*? |
| "How it works" | Hero | Not a conversion CTA |
| "Book Free Demo" | Nav (x2) | Good — clear and specific |
| "Open Savings Calculator" | Calculator section | Good — clear value |
| "Start Saving Today" | Bottom CTA | Implies purchase, not demo |
| "Get My Free Demo" | Modal submit | Good — first person, value-driven |

### Recommendations

1. **Unify the primary CTA.** Use "Book My Free Demo" everywhere (first person). Currently 4 different CTA texts compete.
2. **Hero primary CTA:** Change "See It In Action" → "Book My Free Demo" (direct conversion) or "See How It Works" (if you want a softer ask).
3. **Hero secondary CTA:** "How it works" → "Calculate My Savings" (higher value, links to calculator).
4. **Bottom CTA:** "Start Saving Today" → "Book My Free Demo — See Your ROI in 15 Minutes" (specific, time-bound, matches modal).
5. **Modal button:** "Get My Free Demo" is good. Keep it.

## Before/After Examples

### 1. Primary Headline

**BEFORE:**
> "Your Apprentices Job-Ready in 8 Weeks, Not 6 Months."

**AFTER:**
> "Stop Losing €32K Per Apprentice. Get Them Job-Ready in 8 Weeks."

**WHY:** Leading with the cost of inaction (€32K loss) creates urgency. The current headline is good but passive — it states a benefit without implying a problem to solve.

---

### 2. Subheadline

**BEFORE:**
> "LearnSlice combines IHK exam preparation with your company knowledge into personalized AI learning paths, so your apprentices are job-ready in 8 weeks, not 6 months. Save €32K per apprentice and free up 50% of trainer time."

**AFTER:**
> "AI learning paths that combine IHK exam prep with your company knowledge. Save €32K per apprentice and give your trainers 50% of their week back."

**WHY:** Shorter (29 vs. 46 words). "Give your trainers their week back" is more vivid than "free up 50% of trainer time." Removes redundancy with the headline (8 weeks already stated above).

---

### 3. Primary CTA

**BEFORE:**
> "See It In Action"

**AFTER:**
> "Book My Free Demo"

**WHY:** "See It In Action" is passive and vague. "Book My Free Demo" is specific, uses first person ("My"), includes the value ("Free"), and matches the modal destination. Reduces cognitive friction.

---

### 4. ROI Section Intro

**BEFORE:**
> "€32K–€41K saved per young talent. Big savings, quick ROI."

**AFTER:**
> "€32K–€41K Saved Per Apprentice. Here's Exactly How."

**WHY:** "Big savings, quick ROI" tells the reader what to think (filler). "Here's Exactly How" invites them to verify the claim themselves — more persuasive because it implies transparency.

---

### 5. Meta Description

**BEFORE (current from /companies page):**
> "AI-powered learning platform for apprenticeships. Automate mentoring and save money with LearnSlice."

**AFTER:**
> "Get apprentices job-ready in 8 weeks, not 6 months. LearnSlice combines IHK exam prep with AI coaching — saving €32K per apprentice. Book a free demo."

**WHY:** The current meta is generic (could be any EdTech). The revision includes the specific promise (8 weeks), the mechanism (IHK + AI), the outcome (€32K), and a CTA (book demo). This directly improves SERP click-through rates.
