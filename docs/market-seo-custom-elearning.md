# SEO keyword analysis: custom e-learning software engineering (feeds /solutions)

Goal: one high-performing bilingual blog article (DE primary, EN mirror) that catches decision-makers researching building/commissioning their own e-learning, and funnels them to `/solutions` (`/de/loesungen`). Angle confirmed with stakeholder: **buyer guide with a strong cost/ROI focus** ("E-Learning entwickeln lassen" + "Was kostet E-Learning-Entwicklung").

Method note: research is SERP-based (live DACH + US results, competitor titles, intent, cost figures, PAA-style questions). WebSearch is US-based, so DACH rankings are directional and volumes are qualitative, not tool-verified. If you paste Ahrefs/SEMrush/Keyword Planner data later, I will re-rank on real numbers.

Cannibalization guardrail: `/solutions` and `/de/loesungen` already own the head terms "individuelle E-Learning-Entwicklung" / "custom e-learning development." The blog article must target the informational + cost + "entwickeln lassen" research long-tails and link up to the landing page (hub-and-spoke), not compete for the same head term.

---

## 1. The core strategic finding: two different intents hide under "E-Learning entwickeln lassen"

The DACH SERP splits cleanly into two buyer intents, and they matter because LearnSlice sits in the second one:

- **Intent A: content / course production** ("E-Learning erstellen lassen", WBT, Erklaervideos, Kurse). Commoditized and agency-dense: eCademy, eLearning Werkstatt, visionyou, Knowledge Worker, easylearn. Priced per learning hour / per minute.
- **Intent B: software / platform engineering** ("Lernplattform / LMS / E-Learning-Software entwickeln lassen", Serious Games, Simulationen, KI-Tutoren). This is what `/solutions` sells (AI tutors, custom LMS, apps, simulations, serious games, sovereign/EU-hosted, "you own the IP"). Competitors: wunderwerk, laramate, software-entwickeln-lassen.com, L&K development, innowise, Pfeffermind (games).

Takeaway: the article should lead with Intent B (higher value, less commoditized, matches the product, and where sovereignty/DSGVO/EU-AI-Act + IP-ownership differentiate), while using the cost section to also capture Intent A cost-seekers.

## 2. DACH keyword clusters (PRIORITY)

**Cluster 1 - Commercial "entwickeln lassen" (transactional research):**
- E-Learning entwickeln lassen (broad head, high intent; SERP is content-agency heavy)
- E-Learning-Software entwickeln lassen (software intent, less contested)
- individuelle Lernplattform entwickeln lassen / LMS entwickeln lassen (software intent, best product-fit; laramate + software-entwickeln-lassen.com already rank with near-identical articles = keyword is proven and winnable)
- maßgeschneiderte E-Learning-Lösung / Lernsoftware entwickeln lassen
- E-Learning-Plattform entwickeln lassen

**Cluster 2 - Cost / ROI (secondary, very high buyer intent, blog-friendly SERP):**
- Was kostet E-Learning-Entwicklung / E-Learning entwickeln lassen Kosten
- E-Learning-Plattform Kosten / LMS Entwicklung Kosten
- E-Learning Kosten pro Lernstunde / pro Minute
- SERP here is blog-dominated (tschlotfeldt, M.I.T, inn-ovativ, keelearning, thatworksmedia) = a strong, rankable content opportunity with clear "answer-box" numbers.

**Cluster 3 - Differentiators / niche spokes (low competition, high fit, own these):**
- DSGVO-konforme E-Learning-Entwicklung / E-Learning "made in Germany" / souveraen
- Serious Games / Simulation / KI-Tutor entwickeln lassen (Pfeffermind, eLearning Werkstatt, serious-games.io rank; niche is active)
- eigene Software vs Standard-LMS (build vs buy)

**Observed DACH cost figures (use as the article's answer-box magnets):**
- Content/WBT: ~5.000 bis 20.000 EUR pro Lernstunde; BDVT/VBT Richtwert ~1.000 EUR pro Minute (15.000 bis 25.000 EUR fuer 20 Min).
- Platform/software build: ~13.000 bis 90.000 EUR (one source 20.000 bis 70.000 EUR) depending on tech stack, integrations, customisation.
- Serious games: ab ~15.000 EUR fuer ein Modul; 30.000 bis 80.000 EUR fuer komplette Loesungen. Erstes spielbares Modul in 6 bis 8 Wochen; komplett 3 bis 6 Monate.
- Off-the-shelf LMS reference points: keelearning ab 249 EUR/Monat, iPrendo ab 99 EUR/Monat (use to frame build-vs-buy).

## 3. US keyword clusters (MID priority)

- custom e-learning development / custom e-learning development company / cost / per hour
- bespoke e-learning solutions
- custom e-learning development cost 2026 (pricing guides dominate)

SERP reality: very mature and listicle-heavy with entrenched authority sites (AllenComm, ELB Learning, TrainingPros, check-n-click "Ultimate Pricing Guide", elearningsolutionslab "Buyer's Guide 2026", elearningart calculator). Hard to crack quickly.

**US cost figures:** $5,000 to $50,000 per finished hour (up to $100k+ enterprise); agency ~$100/hr, freelance $30 to $100/hr; Chapman Alliance / Brandon Hall ratio 80 to 300 production hours per 1 finished hour of e-learning (single biggest cost driver = interactivity level).

Recommendation for EN: treat the EN mirror as a long-tail catch + support for the existing `/solutions` page, not a primary battle. Lean the EN article toward the cost/buyer-guide long-tail ("custom e-learning development cost", "how to choose") rather than the ultra-competitive head term.

## 4. Recommended target + article

**Primary DE keyword:** "E-Learning entwickeln lassen" (transactional head) reinforced by cost secondary "Was kostet E-Learning-Entwicklung / E-Learning entwickeln lassen Kosten" and software-fit secondary "individuelle Lernplattform / E-Learning-Software entwickeln lassen."

**Primary EN keyword:** "custom e-learning development cost" reinforced by "custom e-learning development" (guide/long-tail).

**Slugs (avoid cannibalizing /solutions):**
- DE: `/de/blog/e-learning-entwickeln-lassen`
- EN: `/blog/custom-e-learning-development-costs`

**Proposed H2 outline (buyer guide, cost-forward):**
1. Intro: when off-the-shelf stops being enough (the decision, not another feature list)
2. "E-Learning entwickeln lassen": Was das umfasst - Content vs Software/Plattform (define the two intents; position custom software)

8. Fazit + CTA to /de/loesungen
- keyTakeaways (3-5) and FAQ (6-8: was kostet, wie lange dauert es, selbst vs entwickeln lassen, DSGVO, wem gehoert der Code, custom vs Standard-LMS) -> FAQPage schema

**Internal links:** up to `/de/loesungen` (hub, 2-3x), `/de/companies`, and topically to `/de/blog/ki-tools-ausbildung`, `/de/blog/dsgvo-konforme-ki-tools-fuer-ausbilder`. EN mirror -> `/solutions`, `/companies`, EN blog equivalents. Register the pair in `src/utils/page-pairs.mjs`.
