# ICP: DACH Bildungsträger, need-ranked, funding-matched

Targets are ranked by **need intensity** (how hard regulation and operations are forcing a buy now), then matched to a realistic **funding path** so the need can actually be paid for.

## Need drivers (the forces creating demand right now)
- **BFSG (Barrierefreiheitsstärkungsgesetz):** in force since 28 Jun 2025; WCAG 2.1 AA; fines to €100k; hard rebuild cliff 2030. Anyone selling courses electronically is in scope. (Verify before quoting in copy.)
- **AZAV digital-format rule (since 1 Jul 2025):** digital measures must document synchronous instruction; self-learning is logged separately. Forces re-tooling of tracking. (Verify.)
- **SGB IX:** reha providers must offer barrier-free digital learning; their learner groups need adaptive, individualized paths.
- **AI pressure:** strong expectation that LLM assistants become standard for providers within ~3 years; incumbent Moodle/ILIAS lack AI.
- **Funding tailwinds:** ESF Plus 2026/27 explicitly prioritizes AI/digitalization Weiterbildung; BA's 2026 Weiterbildung budget up ~20%.

## Segments

| Priority | Segment | Need | Funding path | Lead offer |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 | Large reha providers (BFW/BBW/RPK) | Very high (SGB IX + BFSG; learners need accessible adaptive paths) | Institutional DRV-funded foundations self-fund; subscription | Accessible, adaptive, managed AI learning platform |
| Tier 1 | Established multi-site AZAV Umschulung/Weiterbildung | High (1 Jul 2025 rule, AZAV reporting gap, AI) | Per-seat opex as Sachkosten; ESF+ co-finance | AZAV-supporting AI platform: synchronous logging, attendance/Verbleib reporting, AI tutor on their content |
| Tier 1 | Private universities (IU, FOM, SRH) | Med-high (scale, AI differentiation, BFSG) | Tuition capex, fast | Custom AI tutor/chatbot layer + integrations |
| Tier 2 | Corporate academies / in-house L&D | Medium (efficiency, no regulatory forcing) | Clean capex (~€21B/yr market, no AZAV ceiling) | Custom white-label AI onboarding + upskilling |
| Tier 2 | Big branded groups (DEKRA, TÜV, bbw, WBS, IBB) | Medium, selective (they build in-house) | Corporate budget | Engineering partner: AI layer, integrations, staff-aug |
| Channel | VHS (~900) + small regional Träger | High need, low/tender-bound budget | Productized SaaS via partners/associations | Self-serve product, not bespoke |

## Disqualifiers / deprioritize
- Open-market AZAV providers living purely on capped per-head rates with no scale (margin-squeezed; Herrenberg-ruling freelancer risk).
- Single-site micro providers (high CAC per logo, thin IT spend).
- Pure tender-bound public bodies as a *first* deal (long cycles) unless a framework (Rahmenvertrag) is in play.

## Buying committee (who to engage, in sequence)
1. **Champion / user:** E-Learning-Verantwortliche, Referent Mediendidaktik, Pädagogische Leitung, writes requirements.
2. **Compliance gate:** QM-Beauftragte (QMB), AZAV-Beauftragte, approves AZAV/QM fit.
3. **Technical gate:** IT-Leitung + Datenschutzbeauftragte, DSGVO veto.
4. **Economic buyer:** Geschäftsführung / Kaufmännische Leitung, owns budget and ROI.

Path: champion → compliance buy-in → technical/DSGVO clearance → economic close.
