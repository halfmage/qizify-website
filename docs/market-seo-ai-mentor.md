# SEO / market analysis: personalised AI mentor (KI-Lernbegleiter) article pair

Goal: audit our existing articles for the "software provider of a personalised AI mentor / AI tutor for
learning" use case, and plan one **bilingual blog article pair** (DE localised for DACH, EN for US) that
catches buyers researching this and funnels them to `/loesungen` (`/solutions`) and the vertical pages.
Hub-and-spoke, same model as the existing `e-learning-entwickeln-lassen` / `custom-e-learning-development-costs`
pair.

**Conversion goal (drives all targeting):** convert readers into customers who want to **build / commission
their own AI mentor**, not readers who want to subscribe to a finished product. Primary ICP order:
(1) learning providers (e-learning/training agencies, Bildungstraeger, edtech), (2) corporate L&D,
(3) universities (procurement-heavy, keep as a section not a lead). This means the article must lead with
**build / "entwickeln lassen" / custom-development intent**, and treat product-category terms
(`KI-Lernbegleiter`, `AI tutor software`) as awareness capture that the article redirects into the build
decision. Leading with a buy-intent category term would pull in subscribe-a-product readers who bounce on
a "we engineer custom" CTA (intent mismatch = the conversion killer).

Method note: SERP-based (live DACH + US results, competitor titles, intent, cost figures, PAA-style
questions). WebSearch is US-geolocated, so DACH rankings/volumes are directional and qualitative, not
tool-verified. Paste Ahrefs/SEMrush/Keyword-Planner numbers later and I will re-rank on real data.

House rules applied: no em/en dashes, never mention the excluded B2C competitor, author "LearnSlice
Team", plain-text keyTakeaways/FAQ. Pricing decision: no LearnSlice rate card on the page; market cost
ranges kept as buyer education, quotes routed to a call.

---

## 0. The one finding that shapes everything: the two markets are NOT symmetric

The DE and EN articles cover the same *topic* but face different *SERP populations*, so the EN post is
not a translation of the DE post. This is the core strategic output.

| | DACH (primary) | US (secondary) |
|---|---|---|
| Who owns the head term ("KI-Tutor" / "AI tutor") | Mixed: B2B vendors + institutions + B2C apps | B2C consumer apps + "best of" listicles (hopeless) |
| Article-winnable intents | "entwickeln lassen" build + cost + Ausbildung vertical (category term = awareness capture) | **Build + cost guide** where dev agencies (not consumer apps) compete |
| Correct article angle | Build buyer-guide: "KI-Lernbegleiter entwickeln lassen, was es kostet, wie Sie einen Anbieter waehlen" | Build buyer-guide: "custom AI tutor development, costs, build vs buy, how to choose a provider" |

Consequence: same topic, same funnel target, and **both articles lead with build/commission intent**
(DE `KI-Lernbegleiter / KI-Tutor entwickeln lassen`; EN `custom AI tutor development`). The product-
category term is captured as a secondary heading and converted via a "build vs buy" section, not led
with. Do not translate the DE hero into the EN post; the two SERPs differ (DE category term is B2B-viable,
the EN one is B2C/listicle territory).

## 1. Audit of our existing articles (the "check the market-seo of our articles" part)

Verdict: our blog already has strong AI-in-Ausbildung coverage, but **no article targets the
AI-tutor/KI-Lernbegleiter *provider / build / cost* intent** as a head keyword (title, H1, slug). That
demand is uncaptured. The relevant existing posts are informational feeders, not competitors, so the
new pair is additive.

| Existing article (EN / DE) | Targets today | Relation to this use case | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| `ai-learning-platform-apprentices` / `ki-lernplattform-azubis` | Informational: AI learning platform for Azubis | Closest topical neighbour; informational, not provider/build intent | **Feeder** into the new pair; add internal link both ways |
| `ai-tools-vocational-training` / `ki-tools-ausbildung` | Informational: AI tools for training | Adjacent; tool-roundup intent | Feeder link |
| `does-ai-learning-help-apprentices` / `lohnt-sich-ki-in-der-ausbildung` | Informational: does AI learning help | Top-of-funnel proof/ROI | Feeder link |
| `ai-in-vocational-training` / `ki-in-der-ausbildung` | Informational: AI in Ausbildung overview | Pillar-ish overview | Feeder link |
| `custom-e-learning-development-costs` / `e-learning-entwickeln-lassen` | Commercial: cost of custom e-learning build | **Cost section overlaps** | Cross-link; keep the new post AI-tutor-specific so the cost angle does not duplicate the general one |

Cannibalization guardrails against our **landing pages** (so the article funnels, not fights):
- `/solutions` / `/de/loesungen` own "custom e-learning development" (AI tutors = 1 of ~12 build items).
  The article links **up** to them and does not claim that head term.
- `/companies`, `/agencies`, `/schools` own the Ausbildung **audience** terms ("AI mentoring",
  "personalised learning"). The article targets the **product-category + build** research intent and
  cross-links to them.

On-page note on the existing feeders: their titles/H1s are Ausbildung-led, none places
"KI-Lernbegleiter / KI-Tutor / AI tutor" in title/H1/slug, so they do not rank for the provider/build
intent. The new pair fills that, and gets internal-link equity from all five feeders above.

## 2. DACH keyword clusters (PRIORITY)

Winnable article structure = a bundle of three adjacent, low-B2B-competition intents that no competitor
combines in one guide: product category + custom build + cost.

**Cluster A - Product category (lead, commercial-investigational):**
- `KI-Lernbegleiter` (primary; B2B vendors VIWIS/Ailentis own it, reads as a category not pure info)
- `KI-Mentor`, `KI-Lerncoach`, `KI-Lernassistent`, `KI-Tutor Software`
- Anchor with a modifier to land in our zone: `KI-Lernbegleiter fuer die Ausbildung` / `... fuer Unternehmen`

**Cluster B - Vertical (best fit, defensible):**
- `KI-Tutor fuer die Ausbildung`, `KI-Tutor fuer Azubis`, `KI-Lernplattform IHK` (LearnSlice already ranks p1)
- Secondary corporate: `KI-Lernassistent fuer Unternehmen`, `KI im Corporate Learning`

**Cluster C - Build / "entwickeln lassen" (transactional, thin field, ideal blog SERP):**
- `KI-Tutor entwickeln lassen`, `KI-Lernplattform entwickeln lassen`, `individuelle Lernplattform entwickeln lassen`
- Competition low-med (laramate, skillbest); captures commission intent.

**Cluster D - Cost (bottom-funnel, near-empty B2B SERP, snippet grab):**
- `KI-Tutor Kosten`, `was kostet ein KI-Tutor`, `KI-Lernplattform Kosten`, `Lernplattform entwickeln lassen Kosten`

**Do NOT target head-on** (B2C-app and listicle swamps): `KI-Nachhilfe`, `KI-Tutor Schule`, bare
`adaptives Lernen`.

**Recommended DE article primary (build intent):** `KI-Lernbegleiter entwickeln lassen` /
`KI-Tutor entwickeln lassen`, reinforced by `was kostet ein KI-Tutor` (build cost).
**Recommended DE secondary (awareness capture, redirect to build):** `KI-Lernbegleiter`,
`KI-Tutor fuer die Ausbildung`, `KI-Lernassistent fuer Unternehmen`.
Rationale: the conversion goal is build/commission customers, so lead with Cluster C + D (commission +
build-cost intent), not the buy-leaning category term in Cluster A.

## 3. US keyword clusters (SECONDARY priority)

US heads are saturated by funded B2C apps (TutorAI, Khanmigo, ai-tutor.ai) and "best of" listicles. A
German secondary-market article cannot win them. The winnable article lane is where **dev-agency blogs**
compete: build + cost.

**Cluster A - Cost guide (best article win, high-value TOFU, competitor-agency-owned today):**
- `how much does it cost to build an AI tutor` (primary), `AI tutor software cost`, `AI tutor development cost`, `cost to build adaptive learning software`

**Cluster B - Build / custom development (transactional guide):**
- `custom AI tutor development`, `build an AI tutor`, `AI tutor development company`, `AI tutoring platform development`

**Cluster C - Vertical / white-label (least contested provider intent):**
- `white-label AI tutor`, `AI tutor for edtech companies`, `AI tutor for corporate training`, `AI tutor for employee onboarding`

**Long-tail matching the client's phrasing:** `personalized AI mentor for corporate training`,
`AI learning companion for [your product]`.

**Do NOT expect to rank** (copy only): `AI tutor`, `AI tutor software`, `personalized AI tutor`,
`AI tutor platform`, `adaptive learning platform`, `best AI tutor`.

**Recommended EN article primary (build intent):** `custom AI tutor development` /
`AI tutor development company`, with `how much does it cost to build an AI tutor` as the strong cost
funnel-filler (best snippet win).
**Recommended EN secondary:** `build an AI tutor`, `white-label AI tutor`, `AI tutor for corporate
training`, `AI tutoring platform development`.

## 4. Cost answer-box data (buyer education, snippet target; no LearnSlice rate card)

Both markets have a wide-open "what does an AI tutor cost" question competitor agencies monetize and no
B2B provider owns cleanly. A market price-range table plus FAQ is a realistic snippet win and a buyer
filter. Keep it AI-tutor-specific so it does not duplicate the general custom-e-learning cost post
(link to that for the general case). Quotes go to a call; these are market ranges, not our prices.

**DACH (EUR):**
- Buy / per-seat SaaS (reference points): consumer AI tutors ~6 to 20/month; small-team platforms from ~99/month; B2B knowledge-bot licenses from ~490/month.
- Build / entwickeln lassen (most relevant): PoC ~5.000 to 15.000; full solution ~30.000 to 200.000+; running ~500 to 5.000/month (model API + infrastructure).

**US (USD):**
- Buy: SaaS $39 to $449/month; enterprise per-seat ~$3 to $100+/user/month; institution-wide managed $10k to $100k/year; enterprise year-1 with LMS integration $85k to $345k.
- Build: MVP (Socratic dialogue + progress) $40k to $80k; custom $25k to $250k+; enterprise $150k to $400k+; maintenance $15k to $100k/year; hosting $500 to $5,000/month.
- White-label vs full custom (competitor-cited): 3-year TCO ~$480k vs ~$3.2M; launch 3 to 6 weeks.

ROI proof points competitors cite (use directionally, do not claim as ours): completion-rate lifts into
the high-80s%, "$120k/year saved in consultant fees", "15+ trainer hours/week saved".

## 5. Recommended article pair

**Slugs (blog convention, register the pair in `src/utils/page-pairs.mjs`):**
- DE: **`/de/blog/ki-lernbegleiter-entwickeln-lassen`** (captures the category term + build intent in one slug)
- EN: **`/blog/custom-ai-tutor-development`**
- `page-pairs.mjs` entry: `'/blog/custom-ai-tutor-development': '/de/blog/ki-lernbegleiter-entwickeln-lassen'`
  (prebuild `check:pairs` enforces it). x-default already points to the DE version.

**Titles (post title / SEO title, 50-60 chars):**
- DE: `KI-Lernbegleiter entwickeln lassen: Kosten, Ablauf, Auswahl`
- EN: `Custom AI Tutor Development: Costs, Build vs Buy, How to Choose`

**H1 (differs from title, keyword-led):**
- DE: `KI-Lernbegleiter entwickeln lassen: was es kostet und wie Sie einen Anbieter waehlen`
- EN: `Custom AI tutor development: what it costs and how to choose a provider`

**Buyer-guide outline (H2s), both lead with the build decision:**
1. Intro: the buyer's real decision (not another feature list): buy a platform, license white-label, or commission a build (frame the build path as the article's spine)
2. Was ist ein KI-Lernbegleiter, und wann lohnt sich ein eigener? / What a custom AI tutor is and when to build your own (40 to 60-word definition = paragraph-snippet target; captures the category term, pivots to build)
3. Fuer wen sich ein eigener KI-Lernbegleiter lohnt / Who builds a custom AI tutor (audience blocks in ICP order: learning providers, corporate L&D, then a short universities note)
4. Kaufen, white-label oder entwickeln lassen? / Buy vs white-label vs build (decision aid; routes build intent to `/solutions`)
5. Was kostet ein KI-Tutor? / What it costs (market ranges table from section 4 + cost drivers = table/list snippet target)
6. Worauf bei der Auswahl achten / How to choose a provider (Didaktik + Engineering, RAG grounding on your curriculum, DSGVO/EU AI Act, IP/data ownership, hosting, maintenance, proof)
7. Souveraenitaet, DSGVO, German hosting (differentiator; strongest in DE, and in US only when the buyer has EU end-users)
8. Fazit + CTA to a strategy call / `/de/loesungen` (`/solutions`)
9. keyTakeaways (3-5, plain text) + FAQ (6-8, plain text) => FAQPage schema

**Featured-snippet targets:** "Was ist ein KI-Lernbegleiter?" and "Was kostet ein KI-Tutor?" (DE);
"how much does it cost to build an AI tutor?" (EN). Question as H2/H3, answer immediately after
(definition 40 to 60 words; cost as a liftable table).

**FAQ / PAA to answer (drives FAQPage schema):**
- DE: Was ist ein KI-Lernbegleiter/KI-Tutor? Was kostet ein KI-Tutor? Wie funktioniert adaptives Lernen
  mit KI? Selbst kaufen oder entwickeln lassen? Wem gehoert der Code / die Daten? DSGVO und Hosting?
  Wie lange dauert die Entwicklung?
- EN: How much does it cost to build an AI tutor? Build vs buy? How long does it take? What ongoing

**Internal links:**
- Up to `/de/loesungen` (`/solutions`) 2-3x (the build hub, primary conversion target)
- Across to `/de/companies`, `/de/schools`, `/de/agencies` (verticals)
- From the five feeder posts in section 1 (add reciprocal links: `ki-lernplattform-azubis`,
  `ki-tools-ausbildung`, `lohnt-sich-ki-in-der-ausbildung`, `ki-in-der-ausbildung`, plus a cross-link
  to `e-learning-entwickeln-lassen` for the general cost case)
- Register the pair in `page-pairs.mjs`; author "LearnSlice Team"; `ctaVariant: "consultation"`

## 6. Open decisions before writing

1. **Structure:** one build-intent pillar per language (recommended, with per-audience blocks) vs a
   small cluster (a build/cost pillar + separate audience spokes for providers / corporate / higher-ed).
   Start with the pillar; add spokes only if a segment pulls.
2. **Lead audience for the pillar:** learning providers (recommended, strongest ICP + cleanest build
   intent) vs corporate L&D. Universities stay a section, not a lead.
3. **Cost content depth:** market ranges + drivers table (recommended, snippet + buyer education, no
   LearnSlice rate card) vs cost mentioned only qualitatively with everything routed to the call.
